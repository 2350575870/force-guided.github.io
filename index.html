Abstract

We present a diffusion-based policy learning framework for robust bipedal locomotion in complex terrains. By formulating action generation as a denoising process conditioned on proprioceptive observations, our method achieves superior stability during command velocity transitions and rapid disturbance recovery. Experiments in Isaac Sim and real-world deployment demonstrate agile traversal of discrete obstacles and autonomous fall recovery, outperforming prior reinforcement learning baselines in sample efficiency and motion naturalness.

Method

Our pipeline consists of three stages: (1) expert trajectory collection via privileged teacher policy, (2) diffusion model training with observation-conditioned denoising, and (3) deployment with DDPM sampling acceleration. The key insight is to leverage the multimodal action distribution modeling capacity of diffusion models for contact-rich locomotion tasks.
